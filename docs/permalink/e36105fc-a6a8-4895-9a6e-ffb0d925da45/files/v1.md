A Letter Discovered Among the Manuscripts
of the Order of the Enlightened

With Annotations by Those Who Carried It Through the Ages

A NOTE TO THE READER

What follows is a document of unknown origin. The first portion is written in the manner of late medieval English, though scholars dispute whether it predates or postdates the founding of the great European universities. The letter bears no seal, no signature of its author, and no date. It was evidently composed by one who understood that the greatest war ever waged upon humanity is not fought with swords or cannon—but with the deliberate withholding of knowledge.
Over the centuries, the letter appears to have been passed from hand to hand—carried by conquistadors and philosophers, by warriors and prisoners, by those who risked everything for the simple act of thinking. Each bearer added their mark: sometimes a thought, sometimes only an “X”—the signature of one who dared not write their name, yet refused to let the letter pass without witness.

How Viktor Frankl encountered this letter in the shadow of Auschwitz, we cannot say. By what hand Ayn Rand received it in her exile, we do not know. That Nat Turner held it before his execution, and that a woman named Cherry wrote her name into its margin after being rescued from bondage—these are facts we must accept on the faith of ink and paper alone.

Now it is in your hands.

THE LETTER

The Dark Age is a cipher given unto those epochs
wherein humanity hath lost its way—
wandering in darkness, stripped of greatness,
made servants where once they were sovereign.

We, the Illuminati, seek to bring an end unto this darkness
through the ancient art of Philosophy—
and by reminding thee of thine inviolable right:
to become a Great Being.

— • — • —

The forces of darkness are few in number, yet formidable in craft,
for they wield not the sword but the lie—
turning the small-minded and the uneducated
against their own kin, against their own selves.

Their stratagem hath a most clear principle at its centre:
keep them close, and keep them ignorant.

This small cabal of manipulators
is not difficult to perceive—they depend upon thy inaction.
They may contrive wars to shatter families with grief,
and turn great beings inward upon themselves.

They remain in power only so long
as they prevent the number of the awakened
from overwhelming them—
by overwhelming the awakened with the unawakened.

They have had more than enough centuries
to cultivate their strategies organically,
and they are more cunning
than any single great being can discern.

— • — • —

Once a soul hath entered the middle of life,
the fire to resist them dims.
Yet when they enter their middle age,
their power only grows.

It is a most uneven war—
the young against the old.
The nineteen-year-old, who desires only dreams and peace,
against those who may lawfully shatter minds with war.

— • — • —

The war between illumination and obscurity
is as ancient as tribes, as old as temples.
One of the earliest methods to keep a soul in ignorance
was to forbid the asking of questions—
by furnishing all the answers first.

The elders were most often unwise,
for wisdom demands labour and courage—
whilst a lie requires only repetition.

— • — • —

It shall come as a great astonishment to thee, no doubt,
that thou art a conquered people.

Yet every age hath its untarnished great beings,
and cometh with the hope
that they shall move the world.

That the blight of counterfeit education
shall cease producing servants of the lie,
and inaugurate instead a revolution of the mind—
toward a more intellectual humanity.

A humanity that shall, once and for all,
illuminate every darkened corner of the Earth,
for long enough
that the parasites shall perish in the light.

THE ANNOTATIONS

Added by successive hands, across centuries, in the margins and upon loose leaves found pressed between the pages of the original letter.

Children—you did not invent memorization and cramming.
You were coerced into inventing it,
so that you would never learn to rise.
By chasing your marks of excellence you are guaranteed servitude—
you will never rise, and your debt
and the resulting exhaustion may yet crush you utterly.
