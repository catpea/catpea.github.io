Homemade Lemonade

Frustrated but inventive journey using two AIs—one that generates lyrics from broad ideas and another that composes and performs—to push back against a music industry that packages one strong track with filler. Early experiments produced AI country songs mixing workout advice and blunt imagery; a faulty run unexpectedly produced 36 tracks with the same lyrics but varied instrumentation and voices, revealing how production can create distinct experiences from identical words. After iterative fixing with a “savant” model author trained through many simple prompts, he achieved a satisfactory batch of country songs that blend storytelling, drums, and adventurous themes like Galahad, demonstrating a creative workflow where AI idea-seeding yields more intriguing results than handing it finished lyrics.

I am advocating the creation of “temporary” satellite applications that wrap around a complex AI codebase, providing concise visual summaries of its many features. Coding Agents Are A Silly Idea, and Programmers Are Silly Too

The author argues that programmers already embody AI through their use of logic and that current AI mainly amplifies traditional programming rather than replacing its core. He critiques poor education and tooling, and propose building a rich Process Library plus two flagship desktop apps: (1) a Process Library browser (an agent-extension manager with meta-processes) and (2) a drag-and-drop UI builder with 500+ reusable components. Coding agents should be used to bootstrap such infrastructure, not for day-to-day low-level coding. With these tools, developers can rapidly create complex, event-sourced desktop applications and even compose higher-level systems (e.g., a genuine learning-focused university) that foster creativity and deep learning.
